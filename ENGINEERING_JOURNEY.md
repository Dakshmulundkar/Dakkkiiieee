## Overview

ForgetMeNot is a multimodal AI-powered dementia care assistant designed to help people suffering from Alzheimer's disease and dementia recognize their loved ones and remember important contextual information in real time.

The project combines face recognition, real-time speech processing, contextual memory retrieval, artificial intelligence, and augmented reality concepts to create a cognitive assistance system.

Unlike traditional reminder applications, ForgetMeNot continuously observes the user's surroundings through a camera and microphone, understands who the patient is interacting with, remembers previous conversations, and provides contextual reminders.

The long-term vision is to integrate this system with AR glasses, allowing patients to receive subtle contextual assistance naturally during conversations.

---

## Motivation

During my exploration of AI applications, I wanted to build something that could genuinely improve someone's quality of life rather than automate an existing workflow.

Many dementia patients struggle to recognize family members, remember conversations, and maintain social relationships.

Current solutions are mostly reminder applications or emergency alert systems.

Very few systems attempt to provide contextual memory assistance in real time.

ForgetMeNot was created to explore how artificial intelligence could augment human memory and provide meaningful assistance to dementia patients.

---

## Problem Statement

Patients with dementia often experience:

• Difficulty recognizing familiar people

• Forgetting recent conversations

• Anxiety during social interactions

• Reduced independence

• Loss of contextual memory

The objective was to design an intelligent assistant capable of recognizing people, understanding conversations, retrieving relevant memories, and presenting information naturally without overwhelming the user.
